# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and tech leads in Florianópolis, SC (and remote), evaluating a junior/internship candidate. They open the site after seeing a LinkedIn or GitHub link. They're scanning for signal in under 60 seconds: does this person ship real things, or just tutorials? Secondary audience: potential freelance clients who need a dev who moves fast.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Guilherme Weiss, 17 years old — developer who already has real products in production (PayReminder, SmartNotes, William Weiss landing page). The site's job is to convert skepticism into conviction. The visitor should leave thinking "this person builds, not just learns."

## Brand Personality

Cinematic. Precise. Unafraid.

The design echoes the maker's attitude: ambitious enough to build a holographic boot sequence at 17, precise enough to ship real products. Not a student portfolio. Not a "learn to code" showcase. A professional who happens to be young.

## Anti-references

- Pastel/cream portfolios with floating islands and rounded-corner icon cards
- "My name is John and I'm a passionate developer" hero sections
- Tutorial-stack showcases (just a todo app)
- LinkedIn-in-a-website (photo + skill bars + testimonial section)
- Tailwind-component-copy aesthetic (Flowbite, ShadCN default, the usual dark-purple glassmorphism starter)

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over claim.** Show what shipped in production, not what the developer knows in theory. Every section earns trust with a concrete deliverable.
2. **Motion as identity.** The cinematic boot sequence and holographic line aren't decoration — they're the first argument for the developer's craft. Motion should be intentional and expensive-feeling.
3. **Restraint in palette, commitment in typography.** One accent color (#06B6D4), one near-black background. The typography and motion do the heavy lifting.
4. **Tension between youth and seriousness.** 17 anos. is stated plainly. The design doesn't apologize for it, and doesn't try to hide it. Confidence without posturing.
5. **Every word earns its place.** The narrative in About is first-person, direct, past-tense deliverables. No fluffy adjectives. This discipline should extend to all copy and UI labels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as target. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected in Framer Motion; extend to GSAP animations. Primary audience uses desktop (recruiter screens), but mobile must not break.
